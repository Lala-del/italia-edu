Entry qualification

High school / secondary education (or higher)

Students must have attended school for at least 12 years.

The entry qualification documents are accepted in the following languages: English / Italian.

If your documents are not in English or Italian, please upload the original documentation or a verified copy of it AS WELL AS a professional translation in English or Italian. You can obtain professional translations either in your country or in Italy, and they can be issued by professional translators, a local court of law or court interpreters.

Entry qualification

English

A minimum B2-level knowledge of English is required for enrolment.

Please make sure you upload one of the certifications we recognise as proof of your English language level.

You can apply to this programme even if you have yet to obtain a language certification, but you will need to provide one in order to complete your enrolment.

Other requirements

This is a limited-admission programme that requires you to take an entry test

If you are a non-EU applicant whose final average mark is equal to or higher than 70% of the maximum achievable mark in the education system in which you obtained your entry qualification, you will be tested via a Skype entry test. If the preliminary evaluation of your documentation by the Counseling and Welcome Unit proves successful, you will be sent instructions for reserving the date and time of your Skype entry test.

Specific requirements for Non-EU applicants:

If you are accepted for enrolment in this programme as a result of a Skype entry test, you will be automatically assigned an Additional Learning Requirement, or ALR, in mathematics (Obbligo Formativo Aggiuntivo, or OFA) to be recovered by the end of your first year of study (deadline of September 30).

In order to take exams in mathematics, statistics, economics and business, you must have already satisfied the ALR by passing an exam. However, if you have a SAT section score of at least 535 out of 800 on the SAT Math test or a GMAT score of at least 42 out of 60 on the GMAT Quantitative test, your ALR in mathematics is automatically satisfied.

Overview

Do you want a career in management that involves working at a commercial or financial institution? This programme equips you with the skill set you need for administration and business management, organisation and staff management, the marketing sector, and the finance and financial mediation sectors.

The Business Administration and Management, which is entirely taught in English, ensures that your degree is international in nature, with its students coming from around the world. And since what happens after you graduate is of prime importance, the programme gets your career off to the right start by including a professional internship that can be carried out either in Italy or abroad.

Career opportunities

As a graduate, you will be ready for employment as a manager or consultant in business, banking, finance, industrial or insurance companies, and enterprises around the world. Career fields include, but are not limited to:

  • Management and supervision in the private sector
  • Human resource management
  • Workplace organisation
  • Marketing
  • Accountancy
  • Finance
  • Sales and distribution
